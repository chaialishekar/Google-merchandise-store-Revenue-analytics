Analysis

What the funnel and the data actually show

Six findings, each one holding up against an obvious alternative explanation.

Conversion-driven, not traffic-driven

Sessions held flat (~2,300–4,600/day); conversion fell 1.73% → 0.89%, Dec to Jan.

The biggest leak sits before checkout

Only 23% of sessions view a product; checkout-to-purchase is a healthy 42%.

The decline is statistically significant

Welch's t-test: t = 4.332, p = 0.00006 — not day-to-day noise.

It's seasonal, not a broken feature

Desktop and mobile conversion move together: r = 0.81.

Paid traffic underperforms every month

CPC converts lowest of all channels, Nov through Jan, without exception.

Returning customers convert 5.3× better

6.67% conversion vs. 1.25% for new customers — the single largest lever.

Statistical Validation

Three tests: is it real, is it isolated, can it be trusted

Every finding above had to survive a specific statistical challenge before it made the cut.

Welch's t-test

"Is the Dec → Jan drop real, or normal daily noise?"
t = 4.332 · p = 0.00006
A gap this size would occur by chance only ~6 times in 100,000 — treated as a real behavioral shift, not noise.

Pearson Correlation

"Isolated to one device, or shared across both?"
r = 0.81
Illustrative pattern — desktop & mobile move together
Rules out a device-specific defect, supports a shared, seasonal cause.

OLS Linear Regression

"Can a simple trend be trusted for forecasting?"
β₁ = −44.27/day
Illustrative shape — green: actual pattern, red: naive straight-line fit
The window includes the Nov/Dec holiday spike, so the slope overstates the decline. Flagged, not used at face value.
Unit Economics

What a customer is worth — and where the math has to stop

This GA4 dataset records behavior, not ad spend, so LTV is real and CAC is a labeled assumption — not a measured result.

Lifetime Value (LTV)

"What is a purchasing customer worth, from real data?"
$89.07 / 3 mo.
Real — total revenue ÷ purchasing customers (4,066). AOV = $69.09; 1.29 orders/customer. Equivalent to $29.69/month per customer.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

"What does it cost to acquire a customer?"
Not computable
This dataset has no marketing spend or cost-per-channel field. Any CAC figure used below is a labeled illustrative assumption, not a measured value.

LTV : CAC & Payback

"Assumes CAC = $25"
3.56 : 1
0.84 mo. payback
Shown only to demonstrate the framework. Above the commonly-cited 3:1 health bar and a sub-1-month payback — but not a claim about this business's actual acquisition cost.
Recommendation

One recommendation followed from the data

The analysis — not the other way around — points to where to intervene.

Recommended intervention: Nova, an AI discovery assistant

Finding #2 identified a pre-checkout leak: most sessions never reach a product page. Nova targets exactly that gap — reducing the friction of finding the right item, for a catalog that's easy to get lost in.
